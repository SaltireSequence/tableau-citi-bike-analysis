Citi Bike Analysis, January 2019 - July 2020

Introduction

Between January 2019 - July 2020 there were over 18 million recorded bike rides on the Citi Bike initative in New York. The objective of this page is to look for interesting trends and anomalies and dicuss those


Data

I decided to use Python to breakdown the downloaded bike data into aggregated dataframes for (i) age-related data (ii) monthly trip-related data; the data I was interested in. Please refer to the associated markdown file found here I soon realised that the timespan I chose to work with was too large (exceeding the 15,000,000 row count of Tableau Public) so took a snapshot of the data instead.


Analysis

Peak Hours

My first observation is that the number of bike rides during peak morning commute time of 8am, for July 2020 vs. July 2019. This isn't really a suprise, given that COVID would have ensured that most people WFH during this time and therefore had little use to Citi Bikes. It's worth noting that the bikes are manily used for recreational use and communting and as both such activities were off-limits due to the lock-down, bikes were otherwise not used as much.


Map

I decided to plot the bike stations and usage vs. GDP per capita (which tableau kindly offers as an overlay), which I thought would make for interesting reading.


Age

Is 1969 the Golden Age?

A very interesting fact that the data showed, is that Customers or Subscribers who are born in 1969 were more than double in volumne than any other age. I double-checked my data, in case I had some how comteminated it and also did research online and yes, 51yr olds are the most popular age of Customer and Subscriber!
